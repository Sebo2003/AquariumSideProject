The "Reef" Of Reef Tanks!

It's not a coral reef without corals, is it? Corals are both surprisingly easy and difficult to care for, and hobbyists have categorized them into three (unscientific) categories based on their skeletal-building behavior: Soft Corals, Large Polyp Stony (LPS), and Small Polyp Stony (SPS). Conveniently, that order also describes how difficult that coral is to care for, where softies are easy and SPS are hard. Like fish, corals have their own size & tank minimums that should be accounted for, but are aggressive like fish as well, due to their stinging sweeper tentacles. Placement refers to where in the tank it ought to be glued (in regards to how close it should be to the light), and flow refers to how much current should be acting upon it (corals need flow so nutrients may be delivered to them).
